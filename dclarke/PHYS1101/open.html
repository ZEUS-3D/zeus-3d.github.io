And that's all she wrote...

Assignment 12 (for "extra-credit") is due midnight on <NEW DATE> Tuesday April14.  Note that while Assignment 12 is entirely optional, material it covers (the laws of Ampère, Lenz, and Faraday) will be covered on the final exam.

I'm available on a drop-in basis any time between now and the exam, normally between 10 and 6.  If you are coming in especially to see me, feel free to e-mail me or call me before hand (coordinates below) to be sure I will be in when you are. 

Don't take the laws of Ampère, Lenz, and Faraday lightly.  They are subtle and require great attention, despite how quickly we rammed through them in class.  There are examples in the practise problems, your text, and in the class notes.  Please go over these carefully and come see me if you need help with them.

The final exam has been scheduled for Thursday, April 23 from 7:00 pm to 10:00 pm (ugh!) in Sobey 255.  You are to have nothing on your desk other than the exam package handed out, and your own pencils and erasers.  As always, a calculator will be provided.  You may also use your "nerd card" if you like, though all needed numbers and formulae will be given on the exam anyway.

The format of the final exam will be six questions of which you must choose five.  Each question will have a part a (a clicker-type question) and a part b (an MP type question).  The exam will cover all material discussed in class, with some emphasis on the E&M unit.

For the purposes of assessing the final grade, I am going to discount the worst assignment of the term.  Thus, if for any reason you missed an assignment, that zero won't count.   If you missed two or more assignments, one or more of your zeroes will count.   By doing the optional assignment 12, you could then effectively eliminate one of those zeroes too.

Technical help for MasteringPhysics: https://247.pearsoned.com (help available 24/7).  A live human: 1-800-667-6337 (9am-9pm M-F, 6pm Sun. to 1am Mon.)

For technical help for clickers, see the ITSS help desk in the Loyola building.  They will either help you fix the problem, or direct you to someone who will.  It is the students' responsibility to ensure that their clickers work!
